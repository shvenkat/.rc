** Math

Assume $x$ exists. Then $x > t$ for all $t \in T$. Hence,

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
e^{i \pi} + 1 &= 0 \\
\int_0^\infty e^{-x^2} \, \mathrm{d}x &= \frac {\pi^\frac{1}{2}} {2}
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
